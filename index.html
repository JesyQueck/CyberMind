CORE PRINCIPLES

  
01 — THINK LIKE THE ATTACKER
To defend, you must understand how systems break
Every day ask: "If I wanted to attack this, how would I?" This single habit is the foundation of the security mindset and separates curious learners from real analysts.

  
02 — EVERYTHING TELLS A STORY
Logs, timestamps and IPs are clues — not noise
A SOC analyst is a digital detective. Train yourself to find patterns: timestamps, login locations, data volumes, protocol anomalies. Every artifact means something.

  
03 — CURIOSITY IS YOUR SUPERPOWER
Ask why it works, not just how to use it
You don't need to memorize everything. You need to know how to think through answers. When something works, ask why. When it breaks, ask how. That instinct is irreplaceable.

  
04 — VERIFY, NEVER ASSUME
Safe-looking is not the same as safe
Attackers blend in. A "normal" login from a known user account can be the start of a breach. Never assume something is benign without checking the context around it.

  
05 — DOCUMENT EVERYTHING
Your journal is proof of growth
Write down what you learn, what confused you, what finally clicked. Your security journal is both a learning accelerator and powerful evidence of skill growth when job hunting.

  
06 — CONSISTENCY BEATS INTENSITY
1 hour daily beats 7 hours on Sunday
Your brain builds security instincts through repetition, not cramming. Show up every day — even 30 focused minutes — and in 6 months you will not recognize how far you have come.
